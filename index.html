Abdul Sattar Edhi

1928 - 2016

Angel of Mercy

Abdul Sattar Edhi (1928-2016) was a revered Pakistani humanitarian, philanthropist, and social worker, widely celebrated for his dedication to helping the poor and needy. Born in Bantva, Gujarat, British India, he migrated to Pakistan after its creation in 1947. Edhi's early experiences with illness and poverty in his family deeply influenced his lifelong commitment to social service. In 1951, he founded the Edhi Foundation in Karachi, starting with a small dispensary. The foundation grew into Pakistan's largest and most respected welfare organization, offering a wide range of services including emergency medical care, orphanages, shelters for the homeless, and rehabilitation centers for drug addicts and mentally ill individuals. The Edhi Foundation also runs the largest fleet of private ambulances in the world, providing free emergency transport. Edhi's work was characterized by his simple lifestyle, unwavering principles, and dedication to humanitarianism regardless of race, religion, or nationality. He received numerous awards and honors for his service, including the Ramon Magsaysay Award for Public Service in 1986. Despite his international acclaim, he remained humble, often rejecting offers of government assistance to ensure the independence and integrity of his foundation. Abdul Sattar Edhi passed away on July 8, 2016, leaving behind a legacy of compassion, selflessness, and an enduring impact on humanitarian efforts in Pakistan and beyond. His life and work continue to inspire millions around the world.

Achievements

Abdul Sattar Edhi, a revered Pakistani philanthropist, social worker, and humanitarian, achieved numerous significant milestones throughout his lifetime. Here are some of his most notable achievements:

  • Edhi Foundation: Founded in 1951, the Edhi Foundation became Pakistan's largest and most respected welfare organization. It operates hospitals, homeless shelters, rehab centers, and orphanages across the country.
  • Ambulance Network: Edhi established the world's largest volunteer ambulance service. Starting with a single vehicle, the service now operates thousands of ambulances across Pakistan, providing emergency medical assistance and transport.
  • Healthcare Services: The Edhi Foundation runs hospitals, clinics, and dispensaries that offer free medical care to the underprivileged. This includes specialized care in maternity and pediatrics.
  • Orphanages and Adoption Services: The Foundation runs several orphanages, providing care and education to abandoned and orphaned children. It also facilitates legal adoption services.
  • Rehabilitation Centers: Edhi set up centers for drug addicts, mentally ill patients, and destitute individuals, offering them rehabilitation and shelter.
  • Funeral and Burial Services: Edhi provided free burial services for unclaimed bodies, ensuring they received dignified burials. The Foundation also operates morgues and a 24-hour emergency service for burials.
